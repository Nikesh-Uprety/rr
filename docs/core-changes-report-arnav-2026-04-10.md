# Core Changes Report for Arnav

Date: 2026-04-10
Project: Rare Atelier storefront and admin
Audience: Non-technical stakeholder update

## Executive Summary

This delivery round focused on making the storefront feel cleaner and more premium, improving the reliability of the admin panel, and making product and image management much easier for future drops.

The biggest outcomes are:

- the storefront now feels more launch-ready and visually consistent
- product and collection management in admin is more flexible
- customer and inventory numbers are more reliable
- product actions in admin are safer and clearer
- image upload and image reuse flows are now much stronger
- a new SS26 collection has been loaded directly from the shared Drive folder into the live product catalog

## Core Business-Facing Changes

### 1. Storefront experience was cleaned up and polished

- The products page was simplified so it focuses more clearly on the products themselves.
- The product grid, hover behavior, navigation alignment, and spacing were refined so the shop feels cleaner and easier to scan.
- A new Gallery page was added to better showcase the brand through visuals, campaign-style imagery, and collab content.
- Storefront menus were tightened up so the experience feels less cluttered and more premium.
- The navigation bar and side menu were visually refined to match the updated storefront style more closely.

### 2. Product layout control was improved in admin

- A dedicated product layout area was added so the team can manage how products are shown on the storefront.
- Category-based display behavior is now easier to control.
- Grid layout and product ordering can be adjusted more intentionally, which will make future collection launches easier to manage.
- This gives the team more control over how products are presented without relying on repeated manual edits.

### 3. Customer and revenue information became more accurate

- Mismatches between customer lifetime spend, order counts, and related revenue views were fixed.
- The customer table was cleaned up so the customer identity and contact information are easier to read.
- Customer detail views now align better with the real order history behind each customer.
- This makes the customer page more trustworthy for business decisions and follow-up work.

### 4. Inventory reporting was corrected and made more reliable

- Inventory now focuses on active products only, instead of mixing in inactive items.
- Cost price, selling price, stock value, and product totals were corrected so they better reflect the actual catalog setup.
- The inventory page now does a better job of showing what is really available and what it is worth.
- This gives a more dependable view of stock, value, and low-stock priorities.

### 5. Product actions in admin are now safer and easier to understand

- Old browser-style warnings were replaced with cleaner confirmation dialogs for actions like delete, archive, activate, and deactivate.
- Product deletion was made safer so items tied to past orders do not break order history.
- Instead of failing badly, products with sales history are now handled in a safer way that keeps business records intact.
- This reduces risk when cleaning up the catalog.

### 6. Image storage and upload flow was stabilized

- Broken product image links were fixed so uploaded images can load correctly again.
- The image storage setup now behaves more consistently from upload through display.
- This reduces image breakage risk across products, buckets, and reused media.

### 7. Google Drive to product import flow is now working

- A new import workflow was added so a public Google Drive folder can be turned into real products in the admin catalog.
- Drive folders can now be pulled into the existing image storage flow instead of requiring manual re-uploading one image at a time.
- This is a major time-saver for future drops and campaign-led launches.

## Overall Business Impact

- The storefront now looks more refined and easier for customers to shop.
- The admin side is more dependable for day-to-day catalog management.
- Reporting around customers and inventory is more trustworthy.
- The team can launch collections faster because media can now move from shared Drive into the live product system much more smoothly.
- The catalog is better prepared for future drops without repeating the same manual setup work.
